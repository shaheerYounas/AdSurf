Phase 1: Automated Market Research & Data Processing  

This phase replaces the manual spreadsheet work. The software will take raw competitor data  
and filter it down to only the most valuable search terms.  

1. Data Collection & Cleaning  

    ●    The Input: The system will prompt the user to upload the raw data file (the CSV export  
         from the competitor research tool) containing the top  10 competitors who have similar  
         designs and prices.  
    ●    The Clean-Up: The software automatically deletes all irrelevant information from the file.  
         It will only keep two pieces of data:  
             1.  Search Volume: How many people search for this term.  
             2.  Organic Rank: Where the competitors naturally show up in the search results.  

2. The Relevance Scoring Engine  

The software will automatically create a new "Relevance Score" column and apply your specific  
grading rule.  

    ●    The Rule: The system looks at the top  10 competitors for a specific search term.  It  
         counts how many of those competitors rank in the top  14 spots (rank <  15).  
    ●    The Score:  It assigns a score from 0 to  10. A score of  10 means all competitors rank  
         highly for that term (highly relevant). A score of 0 means none do (irrelevant).  
    ●    The Filter: The software automatically deletes any search term that scores a 0,  1, or 2.  

3. Automated Verification (The "Manual" Check)  

To narrow the list down further, the software will perform an automated cross-check on Amazon  
to simulate human research.  

    ●    The Action: The system automatically searches Amazon for the remaining terms.  
    ●    The Validation:  It looks at the top  10 to  15 search results.  If 3 to 5 of your original  
         chosen competitors are present in those top results, the search term is marked as  
         "Approved."  If not, it is discarded.  

                                                                                                                 

Phase 2: Automated Campaign Creation  

Once the software has the final list of "Approved" search terms (usually the top 20 to 50), it will  
automatically build the advertising campaigns.  

1. The Naming Convention System  

The software will automatically name every campaign using a standardized template so  
everything remains perfectly organized.  
﻿ Format Component            Description                                  Example  

 Product Name                What you are selling                         CoffeeMaker  

 Ad Type                     Sponsored Product/Brand/Display              SP  

 Targeting                   Auto/Manual/Video                            Manual  

 Match Type                  Exact/Phrase/Broad                           Exact  

 Keyword/Group               The specific word or group number            Coffee Beans or Relevant1  

 Date                        When it was created                          May 11  

2. Building the "Hero" Campaign  

The software isolates the absolute most important search term and creates a dedicated,  
aggressive campaign j ust for it.  

    ●   Structure:  1 Campaign >  1 Ad Group >  1 Keyword.  
    ●   Budget: Set automatically to $10.  
    ●   Bidding: The software sets the bid at $1.00 (or pulls Amazon’s suggested estimated  
        bid).  

3. Building the Grouped Campaigns (Top 20-50 Terms)  

The software divides the remaining approved terms into small batches of 5 to 7 words.  It then  
creates three distinct types of campaigns for each batch.  

    ●   Exact Match Campaigns:  
             ○   Rule: The ad only shows if the customer types these exact words.  
             ○   Budget: $10 per campaign.  
    ●   Phrase Match Campaigns:  
             ○   Rule: The ad shows if the customer types the phrase, even with other words  
                 before or after it.  
             ○   Budget: $10 per campaign.  
﻿     ●   CRITICAL AUTOMATION: The software will automatically take the words from the  
         Exact Match campaign and block them here (Negative Exact). This ensures the Phrase  
         campaigns don't steal traffic from the Exact campaigns.  
     ●   Broad Match Campaigns:  
             ○    Rule: The ad shows if the customer types words related to your terms, in any  
                  order.  
             ○    Budget: $10 per campaign.  
     ●   CRITICAL AUTOMATION: The software will automatically take the words from the  
         Phrase Match campaign and block them here (Negative Phrase). This forces the Broad  
         campaign to only hunt for completely new, undiscovered search terms.  

                                                                                                                    

Phase 3: The 14-Day Automated Monitoring System  

The software will now watch the campaigns and make mathematical adj ustments without  
emotional bias.  

1. The First 7 Days (Budget Consumption Check)  

     ●   The software monitors the $10 daily budget for every campaign.  
     ●   The Adj ustment:  If a campaign is not spending its full $10, it means the bid isn't high  
         enough to win ad space. The software will automatically increase the bid for those  
         specific search terms by 10%.  It will repeat this daily until the budget is being consumed.  

2. Day 7 Evaluation (The Profitability Check)  

     ●   After exactly 7 days, the software calculates the ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sales—how  
         much was spent on ads compared to the sales generated).  
     ●   The Decision:  If the ACOS is less than 50%, the software locks the campaign and  
         makes zero changes.  It allows the campaign to run untouched for another 7 days (Days  
         8 through  14) to gather reliable, long-term shopping data before attempting any complex  
         optimization.
